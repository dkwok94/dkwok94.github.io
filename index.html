Hello. I'm Derek.

I was born in 1994 in Chicago, Illinois and from an early age, I was always interested in science and technology. In school, my favorite subjects were mathematics, chemistry, and physics because I was interested in learning about the fundamentals behind how things worked. This drove me to study materials science and engineering in college because of the huge role that materials play in all of science and engineering. It was a fascinating topic that really opened my eyes to the world around me and how complicated even the simplest products and technologies are at the atomic level.

Graduating college in 2016, I worked in the materials industry for about a year and a half doing research and development and manufacturing work. During this time, I came across many issues in terms of data analysis for projects that would have easily been solved if I had coding skills. One day, I decided to try and code a script that would automatically separate the data I needed from a huge set of raw data from a manufacturing trial. It was so much fun and the feeling of accomplishment as I pressed the "run" button and watched a data sheet populate with relevant data drove me to study more on programming. I did some work in Javascript and realized that I wanted to pursue software as a career avenue and I applied to various programs, where I got into Holberton School in San Francisco.

I am really enjoying my experiences so far and have since coded a printf function, a simple shell, a Monty 0.98 command interpreter, and a console for a clone of AirBnB. I find myself interested in the field of machine learning and have since started a Coursera course on the subject to learn more about the algorithms and concepts behind the discipline.

Aside from coding, I enjoy music and foreign languages. I have played violin since I was 5 and piano since I was 8. I am a huge fan of classical music and thoroughly enjoy the works of Chopin, Tchaikovsky, Beethoven, Mahler, Rachmaninoff, Brahms, and dozens of others. As a kid, I was part of a performance group that went on a few tours of Europe and Asia that allowed me to see different parts of the world. This inspired my interest in foreign languages and connecting with people from around the world. One of my life goals is become a polyglot of 10 languages. Currently, I know a little Cantonese, intermediate French, and intermediate Spanish, extremely basic German, and I look forward to learning more! As a result, I enjoy traveling and experiencing new cultures and people, and their food - I love food!
