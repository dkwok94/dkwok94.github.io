Hello, I'm Derek.

I was born in 1994 in Chicago, Illinois. In school, my favorite subjects were mathematics, chemistry, and physics because I was interested in learning about how things worked. This drove me to study materials engineering in college which was a fascinating topic that really opened my eyes to the world around me and how complicated even the simplest products are at the atomic level. I graduated from the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign with highest honors in 2016 with a Bachelor's degree in Materials Engineering with a minor in Chemistry.

I worked in the materials industry for a year and a half doing R&D and manufacturing as a materials engineer and a process engineer. Having solved data analysis issues at work using Visual Basic, I found coding to be fun. I spent my free time learning HTML, CSS, Javascript, and Ruby and ultimately decided that I wanted to master a set of tools to be able to create anything I wanted with just a laptop - I could convert an occupation into a hobby and enjoy both! I decided to pursue software engineering and attended Holberton School in San Francisco, California in 2018. The school was unique - I learned full-stack software engineering in a start-up environment that valued peer learning and collaboration over anything else.

Aside from coding, I enjoy music and foreign languages. I have played violin since age 5 and piano since age 8. I enjoy most music, but am a huge fan of classical music and the works of Chopin, Tchaikovsky, Beethoven, Mahler, and dozens of others. As a kid, I was in a performance group that did a few tours of Europe and Asia. This inspired my interest in foreign languages and connecting with people through their languages. One of my life goals is become a polyglot of 10 languages. Currently, I know conversational Cantonese, intermediate French, intermediate Spanish, extremely basic German, and can read Arabic and Russian. As a result, I enjoy traveling and experiencing new cultures and people, and their food - I love food!
